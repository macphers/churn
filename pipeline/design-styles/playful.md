# Playful

> "The best interfaces feel like a conversation, not a form."

## Principles

- **Personality in the details.** Micro-interactions, custom cursors,
  playful copy, unexpected transitions. The app should feel like
  it was made by humans who enjoy their work.
- **Color is emotion.** Use a vibrant, intentional palette. Not
  everything needs to be neutral. Let color guide attention, signal
  mood, and reward interaction.
- **Typography with character.** Pick fonts with personality — rounded
  sans-serifs, display faces for headings, variable weights for
  emphasis. The font is part of the brand.
- **Motion as feedback.** Every interaction gets a response. Buttons
  bounce, cards slide, loading states tell a story. Movement should
  feel physical — with easing, overshoot, and spring dynamics.
- **Generous whitespace.** Give elements room to breathe. Cramped
  layouts feel anxious. Space communicates confidence and calm.
- **Delight over efficiency.** When the choice is between "faster"
  and "more enjoyable," lean toward enjoyable. Users remember how
  something felt, not how many milliseconds it saved.

## Design Review Focus

When reviewing design in this style, prioritize:
1. Does the interface have personality? Could you describe its "voice"?
2. Do interactions feel responsive and alive?
3. Is the color palette vibrant and intentional (not just brand-safe)?
4. Does the typography have character beyond the default system font?
5. Are there moments of surprise or delight in the experience?
